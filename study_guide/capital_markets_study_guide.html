Capital Markets and Stock Exchanges Study Guide

Comprehensive Questions and Answers

  • Question 1: What is a capital market?

    Answer: A capital market is a financial market where long-term debt or equity-backed securities are bought and sold. These markets are used by companies, governments, and other organizations to raise funds for operations, projects, or growth.

  • Question 2: What are the two main types of capital markets?

    Answer: The two main types of capital markets are primary markets, where new securities are issued, and secondary markets, where existing securities are traded among investors.

  • Question 68: What is a stock exchange?

    Answer: A stock exchange is a regulated marketplace where securities, such as stocks and bonds, are bought and sold. Stock exchanges facilitate the trading of financial instruments for publicly listed companies.

  • Question 69: What role do stock exchanges play in capital markets?

    Answer: Stock exchanges provide a platform for companies to raise capital through the issuance of stocks and bonds, and they offer investors a mechanism to buy and sell these securities, contributing to market liquidity and price discovery.

Cheatsheet

Key Terms:

  • Capital Markets: Markets for buying and selling equity and debt instruments.
  • Stock Exchange: A platform for trading securities.
  • Liquidity: The ease with which an asset can be converted into cash.
  • IPO: Initial Public Offering, the first sale of a company’s stock to the public.
  • Market Capitalization: The total value of a company’s outstanding shares.
